# Thai Market Context — Portfolio Reference

Specific rules and context for Thai investors with US/mixed portfolios.

## Withholding Tax on US Dividends

| Investor Type | Rate | Recoverable |
|--------------|------|-------------|
| Thai individual (no treaty claim) | 30% | No |
| Thai individual (W-8BEN filed) | 15% | Partially via PND.90 |
| Thai-domiciled ETF (e.g., KFVRMF) | 0–15% | Handled by fund |

**Practical rule:** US stocks held directly → 15% WHT with W-8BEN. Thai-domiciled funds → fund handles tax, simpler for Thai investors.

## FX Impact (USD/THB)

- Portfolio value in THB = USD value × USD/THB rate
- When THB strengthens (rate drops): USD assets lose THB value
- Rule of thumb: 1% THB strengthening = -1% portfolio value in THB terms
- Hedge consideration: Only worthwhile for >12-month positions with large FX exposure

## Thai Tax-Advantaged Funds

| Fund Type | Annual Cap | Tax Deduction | Lock-in |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| RMF | 30% of income, max ฿500K | Yes (within 500K total) | Until 55 + 5yr |
| ThaiESG | 30% of income, max ฿300K | Yes (separate limit) | 8 years |
| SSF | 30% of income, max ฿200K | Yes (within 500K total) | 10 years |

**RMF/ThaiESG priority:** Use before buying US stocks directly if tax bracket > 20%

## SET vs US Market Comparison

| Factor | SET | US Market |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| Dividend yield avg | 3–4% | 1.3% |
| P/E avg | 15–18x | 22–25x |
| Growth rate | Lower | Higher |
| Currency risk | Base (THB) | USD exposure |
| Liquidity | Lower | High |
