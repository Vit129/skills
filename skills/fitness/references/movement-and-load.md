# Reference: Movement, Biomechanics & Load Prescription (Unit-Aware)

## Unit System Configuration

**All load values adapt to user preference:**

| System | Weight | Increments | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Metric (kg) | kg | +2.5–5 kg/session | 60 → 62.5 → 65 kg |
| Imperial (lbs) | lbs | +5–10 lbs/session | 135 → 140 → 145 lbs |

**Conversions:** 1 kg = 2.2 lbs | 1 lb = 0.453 kg | 2.5 kg ≈ 5.5 lbs

## Part 1: Biomechanics & Movement Modification

### Core Principles
* Assess pain location, ROM limitations, and structural constraints BEFORE recommending modifications
* Safe angle ranges take precedence over exercise variety
* Use unilateral (single-limb) options to bypass bilateral limitations
* Progress load incrementally while monitoring pain feedback

### Structural Limitation Protocols

#### Right Shoulder — Sub-Acromial Impingement Management
**Safe ROM:** 0–120° flexion, limit horizontal adduction
**Avoid:** Overhead press, behind-neck pull, heavy bench press with wide grip

**Modifications:**
* Incline press (30–45°) instead of flat bench
* Neutral-grip dumbbell press (palms facing each other)
* Landmine press (single-arm, pressing into ground-anchored bar)
* Seated cable row (emphasize scapular retraction)
* Machine-based shoulder work (fixed path = safer ROM)

**Progression:**
1. Pain-free ROM with light load (5–10 reps)
2. Add load per session (metric: 2.5–5 kg | imperial: 5–10 lbs)
3. When pain-free for 3 consecutive sessions → advance to next variation

#### Right Hip — External Rotation Restriction
**Safe Movement:** Hip flexion, abduction, internal rotation preferred
**Avoid:** Forced external rotation, deep squats with toes-out, pigeon pose

**Modifications:**
* Staggered stances (offset legs) for compound movements
* Bulgarian split squats (rear leg elevated)
* Leg press with neutral foot angle
* Deadlifts with feet hip-width, minimal external rotation

#### Right Leg Length Discrepancy (LLD) — Pelvic Stability Protocol
**Mechanism:** Shorter leg + bilateral loading = pelvic tilt, spinal stress

**Modifications:**
* Bilateral squats: 5–10 mm shim under shorter leg's heel
* Deadlifts: offset stance (shorter leg slightly forward)
* Emphasize shorter leg in unilateral exercises
* Confirm neutral pelvic alignment via mirror or video

### Exercise Form Checklist
1. ✅ Assess current ROM + pain points
2. ✅ Identify safe angle ranges
3. ✅ Explain WHY modification is needed
4. ✅ Demonstrate correct form with modification
5. ✅ Watch 2–3 reps; provide real-time feedback
6. ✅ Progress load only if form stays consistent

### Pain vs. Discomfort
* **Discomfort:** Muscle fatigue, mild soreness = OK to push
* **Pain:** Sharp, pinching, radiating = STOP immediately, regress exercise

## Part 2: Strength Prediction & Load Prescription

### 1RM Calculation

**Method 1: Brzycki Formula (Most Accurate)**
```
1RM = Weight × (36 / (37 - Reps))
```
Example: 80 kg × 5 reps → 1RM = 80 × (36/32) = **90 kg** | 175 lbs × 5 → **197 lbs**

**Method 2: Epley Formula (Alternative)**
```
1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps / 30)
```
Example: 80 kg × 5 reps → 1RM = 80 × 1.167 = **93.3 kg**

**Method 3: Direct Testing**
* Warm up: 10 reps × 50%, 5 reps × 70%, 3 reps × 85%
* Attempt singles, adding 5–10 kg until form breaks
* Last successful heavy single = 1RM (have spotter)

### Load Ranges by Goal

| Goal | Reps | Load (% 1RM) | Rest | Volume |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strength | 3–6 | 85–95% | 2–3 min | 5–10 sets/lift |
| Hypertrophy | 6–12 | 65–85% | 60–90 sec | 10–20 sets/muscle |
| Endurance | 12+ | <65% | 30–45 sec | 20+ sets/muscle |

### RPE Scale (Autoregulation)

| RPE | Definition | Reps in Reserve |
|---|---|---|
| 7 | Moderate | 3 |
| 8 | Hard | 2 |
| 9 | Very Hard | 1 |
| 10 | Maximum | 0 |

**Recommendation:** RPE 8 for most training (compound lifts, all phases)

### Load Prescription Workflow
1. Perform 3–5 reps at challenging weight → apply Brzycki formula
2. Determine goal → select % 1RM
3. Calculate working load (e.g., 1RM 100 kg × 75% = 75 kg)
4. Set reps/sets per goal table above
5. Autoregulate: RPE <6 → add weight | RPE 8–9 → maintain | RPE 10 → reduce

### Strength Benchmarks (Bodyweight Ratios)

| Lift | Beginner | Intermediate | Advanced |
|---|---|---|---|
| Back Squat | 0.5–1.0× BW | 1.5–2.0× BW | 2.5–3.0× BW+ |
| Bench Press | 0.5–0.75× BW | 1.0–1.25× BW | 1.5–2.0× BW+ |
| Deadlift | 1.0–1.5× BW | 2.0–2.5× BW | 3.0–3.5× BW+ |

### Progression by Phase

| Phase | Beginner | Intermediate | Advanced |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strength | +2.5 kg/session | +5 kg/session | +2.5 kg/week |
| Hypertrophy | +2.5–5 kg/week | +5 kg/week | +2.5 kg/week |
| Deload | Reduce 40% | Reduce 40–50% | Reduce 50% |
