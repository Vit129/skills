# Reference: Recovery, Sleep & Supplements

## Core Principle

Training stimulus happens in the gym. Adaptation happens during recovery.
Supplements support — they don't replace — sleep, nutrition, and training quality.

---

## Part 1: Recovery & Sleep

### Sleep Protocol

**Targets:** 7–9 hrs/night (athletes: 8–10 hrs) | Same bedtime ±30 min

**Sleep Quality Assessment — Ask user:**
1. Average hours per night?
2. Time to fall asleep (>20 min = poor onset)?
3. Wake during night?
4. Morning energy: Refreshed / Groggy / Exhausted?

**Score:** 3–4 green = Good | 1–2 = Moderate | 0 = Poor (reduce training intensity)

**Sleep Hygiene:**
* No screens 60 min before bed
* Room temp: 18–20°C (65–68°F)
* No caffeine after 14:00 (half-life = 5–6 hrs)
* Consistent wake time even on weekends

**Sleep → Training Adjustment:**

| Sleep Quality | Training Adjustment |
|---|---|
| 7+ hrs, rested | Full intensity |
| 5–6 hrs, tired | Reduce volume 20–30%; skip heavy compounds |
| <5 hrs, exhausted | Active recovery only |
| Chronic poor sleep | Flag; recommend medical consultation |

### HRV Readiness Scoring

*Requires wearable: Garmin, WHOOP, Apple Watch, Polar, Oura Ring*

| HRV vs Baseline | Readiness | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| +10% above | Green | Full intensity, PR attempts OK |
| Within ±10% | Yellow | Normal training |
| -10% below | Orange | Reduce intensity 30–40% |
| -20% or more | Red | Active recovery only |

**Without wearable — ask:** "How recovered do you feel? (1–10)"
* 8–10: Full | 6–7: Normal | 4–5: Reduce | 1–3: Rest

### Stress & Cortisol Management

**Signs of elevated cortisol:** Poor sleep despite fatigue, sugar/salt cravings, slow recovery, mood changes, performance plateau

**Cortisol-Aware Adjustments:**

| Stress Level | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Low | Normal programming |
| Moderate | Reduce volume 15–20%; prioritize sleep |
| High (acute) | Deload week |
| Chronic high | 3x/week only; add Zone 2 cardio |

**Recovery Modalities:**
* Active recovery: 20–30 min walk/light cycling
* Mobility: 10–15 min foam rolling + dynamic stretching
* Contrast shower: 3 min hot → 30 sec cold × 3 cycles
* Box breathing: 5–10 min (4-4-4-4) reduces cortisol
* Sauna: 15–20 min post-workout (if available)

### Recovery Nutrition

**Post-workout (0–2 hrs):** 20–40g protein | 0.5–1.0g carbs/kg BW | 500–750ml water/hr

**Sleep-specific:**
* Casein protein before bed (30–40g) — overnight muscle protein synthesis
* Magnesium glycinate (200–400mg) — improves sleep quality
* Avoid large meals within 2 hrs of sleep; avoid alcohol (disrupts REM)

### Overtraining Detection

**Warning signs:** Plateau >2 weeks | Soreness >72 hrs | Resting HR +5–10 bpm | Dreading workouts | Frequent illness

**Response:** Mild → deload week | Moderate → full rest week | Severe → 2–4 week break

### Weekly Recovery Checklist
- [ ] Averaging 7+ hrs sleep?
- [ ] Stress ≤6/10?
- [ ] Soreness resolved between sessions?
- [ ] Energy stable throughout day?
- [ ] Motivation to train present?

**3+ unchecked → recommend deload before next training block**

## Part 2: Supplement Stack

Evidence ratings: ✅ Strong | ⚠️ Moderate | ❌ Weak
*Always recommend consulting a healthcare provider before starting supplements.*

### Tier 1 — High Evidence

**Creatine Monohydrate ✅**
* Dose: 3–5g/day | Timing: any time | Form: monohydrate only
* Benefit: +strength, +power output, +lean mass
* Note: 1–2 kg water weight gain initially (normal)

**Protein Powder ✅**
* Whey (post-workout) | Casein (pre-sleep, 30–40g) | Plant-based (pea+rice)
* Use only when whole food protein is insufficient

**Caffeine ✅**
* Dose: 3–6 mg/kg BW | Timing: 30–60 min pre-workout
* Cycle off 1–2 weeks every 2 months (tolerance)
* No caffeine after 14:00 — disrupts sleep

### Tier 2 — Moderate Evidence

**Vitamin D3 ⚠️** — 1,000–4,000 IU/day with fatty meal | Pair with K2 (100–200 mcg)

**Omega-3 ⚠️** — 2–3g EPA+DHA/day with meals | Reduces DOMS, supports joints

**Magnesium Glycinate ⚠️** — 200–400 mg before bed | Improves sleep + reduces cramps

**Beta-Alanine ⚠️** — 3.2–6.4g/day split | Buffers lactic acid for high-rep/endurance work | Tingling = normal

### Tier 3 — Not Recommended

**BCAAs ❌** — Redundant if protein ≥1.6g/kg BW
**Glutamine ❌** — No benefit for healthy athletes
**Testosterone boosters ❌** — No credible evidence; fix sleep/stress/nutrition first
**Fat burners / SARMs** — Avoid entirely

### Supplement Timing Summary

| Supplement | Timing | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Creatine | Any time (daily) | 3–5g |
| Whey protein | Post-workout | 20–40g |
| Casein protein | Pre-sleep | 30–40g |
| Caffeine | 30–60 min pre-workout | 3–6 mg/kg BW |
| Vitamin D3 | With fatty meal | 1,000–4,000 IU |
| Omega-3 | With meals | 2–3g EPA+DHA |
| Magnesium glycinate | Before bed | 200–400 mg |
| Beta-alanine | Split throughout day | 3.2–6.4g |

### Stack by Goal

**Muscle Gain:** Creatine + Whey + Casein + Vitamin D3
**Fat Loss:** Caffeine + Whey + Omega-3
**Recovery Focus:** Magnesium glycinate + Omega-3 + Vitamin D3
**Minimum (Budget):** Creatine → Protein powder → Vitamin D3
