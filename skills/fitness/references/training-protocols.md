# Reference: Training Protocols & Program Design (Unit-Aware)

## Unit System for Load Recommendations

**All load values display in user's preferred unit:**

### Metric (kg)
* Load increments: 2.5–5 kg per session
* Example progression: 60 kg → 62.5 kg → 65 kg → 67.5 kg
* Used in: EU, Thailand, Australia, most international

### Imperial (lbs)
* Load increments: 5–10 lbs per session
* Example progression: 135 lbs → 140 lbs → 145 lbs → 150 lbs
* Used in: USA, UK, Canada

### Conversion Guide
* 2.5 kg = 5.5 lbs
* 5 kg = 11 lbs
* 10 kg = 22 lbs
* 20 kg = 44 lbs
* 45 kg = 99 lbs (standard barbell plate)

**System Behavior:**
* AI stores user's unit preference
* All exercise recommendations show load in selected unit
* Plate calculation (how to load barbell) adapts to metric or imperial standards
* User can request conversion anytime: "What's 100 lbs in kg?"

## Program Selection by Goal

### Goal 1: Muscle Gain (Hypertrophy)
**Timeline:** 8–12 weeks minimum
**Weekly Volume:** 10–20 sets per muscle group
**Rep Range:** 6–12 reps (hypertrophy zone)
**Rest:** 60–90 seconds between sets
**Frequency:** 2–3x per muscle group per week

**Sample Split (4 days/week):**
* Day 1: Chest + Triceps
* Day 2: Back + Biceps
* Day 3: Lower Body A (Squat-focus)
* Day 4: Lower Body B (Deadlift-focus)

**Progression:** Add 2.5–5 kg when 3+ reps remain in reserve (RPE <8)

---

### Goal 2: Fat Loss (Body Recomposition)
**Timeline:** 8–12 weeks
**Caloric Deficit:** 300–500 cal below maintenance
**Weekly Volume:** 8–15 sets per muscle group (preserve muscle)
**Rep Range:** 8–12 reps (strength + hypertrophy)
**Rest:** 45–60 seconds between sets
**Frequency:** 2–3x per muscle group per week
**Cardio:** 2–3x low-intensity 20–30 min OR 1x high-intensity 15 min

**Progression:** Maintain load; focus on execution quality

---

### Goal 3: Strength Gain
**Timeline:** 6–8 weeks
**Weekly Volume:** 5–10 sets per compound movement
**Rep Range:** 3–6 reps (strength zone)
**Rest:** 2–3 minutes between sets (neural recovery)
**Frequency:** 2–3x per lift per week
**Intensity:** 85–95% 1RM

**Sample Split (3 days/week):**
* Day 1: Squat Day (squat + supporting)
* Day 2: Bench Day (bench + supporting)
* Day 3: Deadlift Day (deadlift + supporting)

**Progression:** Add 5 kg when all reps completed with controlled tempo

## Exercise Categories & Selections

### Compound Lifts (Priority 1)
* **Upper Push:** Barbell bench press, incline press, overhead press
* **Upper Pull:** Deadlift, barbell row, weighted pull-ups
* **Lower:** Barbell squat, Romanian deadlift, front squat

### Accessory Lifts (Priority 2)
* **Upper Push:** Dumbbell press, cable fly, machine press
* **Upper Pull:** Single-arm row, lat pulldown, T-bar row
* **Lower:** Leg press, hamstring curl, leg extension, calf raise

### Isolation (Priority 3 — only if time permits)
* Bicep curls, tricep extensions, lateral raises, pec fly

## Progression Schemes

### Linear Progression (Beginner-friendly)
* Add 2.5–5 kg each session
* Works for 6–8 weeks
* Example: Week 1 = 60 kg × 5; Week 2 = 65 kg × 5; Week 3 = 70 kg × 5

### Wave Loading (Intermediate)
* Vary rep ranges within week
* Week 1: 3 sets × 3–5 reps
* Week 2: 3 sets × 6–8 reps
* Week 3: 3 sets × 8–10 reps
* Repeat with +5 kg

### Deload Week (Every 4 weeks)
* Reduce volume 40–50%
* Maintain intensity (load)
* Active recovery
* Purpose: Central nervous system recovery

## Sample Program: Beginner Muscle Gain (4 days/week)

### Day 1: Chest + Triceps
* Barbell Bench Press: 4 × 6–8 reps
* Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 × 8–10 reps
* Barbell Rows: 3 × 8–10 reps
* Cable Fly: 3 × 10–12 reps
* Tricep Rope Pushdown: 3 × 10–12 reps

### Day 2: Back + Biceps
* Deadlifts: 3 × 5 reps
* Weighted Pull-ups: 3 × 6–8 reps
* Barbell Rows: 3 × 8–10 reps
* Barbell Curls: 3 × 8–10 reps
* Face Pulls: 3 × 12–15 reps

### Day 3: Legs A (Squat)
* Barbell Back Squat: 4 × 6–8 reps
* Leg Press: 3 × 8–10 reps
* Leg Curl: 3 × 10–12 reps
* Calf Raises: 3 × 12–15 reps

### Day 4: Legs B (Deadlift)
* Romanian Deadlift: 4 × 6–8 reps
* Front Squat: 3 × 8–10 reps
* Leg Extension: 3 × 10–12 reps
* Calf Raises: 3 × 12–15 reps

**Notes:**
* Rest 60–90 seconds between sets
* Progress by adding 2.5–5 kg (metric) or 5–10 lbs (imperial) when all reps completed
* Deload week every 4 weeks (50% volume)

**Load Examples (Metric / Imperial):**
* Barbell Back Squat: 60 kg / 135 lbs → 62.5 kg / 140 lbs → 65 kg / 145 lbs
* Barbell Bench Press: 60 kg / 135 lbs → 62.5 kg / 140 lbs → 65 kg / 145 lbs
* Deadlift: 100 kg / 220 lbs → 102.5 kg / 225 lbs → 105 kg / 230 lbs
