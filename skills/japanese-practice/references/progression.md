# Japanese Practice — Progress Tracking & Levels

## Difficulty Levels

**Level 1 (Start here):**
- Simple present/past (～ます / ～ました)
- Basic particles (は、を、に)
- Hiragana/Katakana fluency

**Level 2:**
- Te-form (～て) for connecting sentences
- Continuous aspect (～ています)
- Conditional forms (～たら、～ば)
- Kanji reading (N4 level ~100 kanji)

**Level 3:**
- Polite keigo (敬語) — respectful language
- Causative/passive (～させる、～られる)
- Advanced particles and nuance
- Kanji mastery (N3 level ~300+ kanji)

---

## Assessment Rubric

| Criterion | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Accuracy** | Mostly correct (1-2 errors per sentence) | Few errors (<1 per sentence) | Nearly flawless (0 errors) |
| **Grammar Range** | Only basic tenses (～ます, ～ました) | Multiple tenses + some particles | Advanced forms (causative, passive) |
| **Particle Usage** | Often wrong particles | Mostly correct particles | Particle nuances understood |
| **Kanji/Writing** | Hiragana mostly; few kanji | Basic kanji reading; some writing | N4+ kanji reading/writing |
| **Fluency** | Word-by-word; choppy | Natural phrase structure | Native-like flow |
| **Vocabulary** | Basic 100-200 words | Intermediate 300-500 words | Advanced 1000+ words |

**Scoring:** "Level 1+0.5 → improving particles" or "Strong Level 2 grammar, weak particle accuracy."
