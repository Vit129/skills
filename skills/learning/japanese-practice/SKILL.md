# Japanese Language Practice Skill

**Purpose:** Help you practice Japanese writing (hiragana/katakana/kanji) and grammar with immediate corrections and cultural context.

## How It Works

1. Write in Japanese — describe your day, explain a concept, ask questions, or translate from English/Thai
2. I'll provide:
   - **Correction:** Proper spelling/grammar/punctuation
   - **Breakdown:** Kanji readings, grammar patterns, particles explained
   - **Natural alternative:** How native speakers might say it
   - **Cultural note:** Context or nuance (when relevant)
   - **Next challenge:** Gradually increase difficulty

## Format

```
**Correction:** [Fixed sentence with furigana if needed]

**Breakdown:**
- [Word/Grammar point explanation]
- [Particle usage]
- [Kanji reading if applicable]

**Natural way to say it:** [Native speaker version]

**Pattern:** [Reusable grammar rule]

**Next level:** [Challenge — try X next time]
```

## Examples

**You write:**
私はプログラマーです。毎日コードを書きます。

**I respond:**
**Status:** Both forms are correct! ✓

**Breakdown:**
- **私** (わたし) = I
- **プログラマー** = programmer (katakana for foreign words)
- **毎日** (まいにち) = every day
- **コードを書きます** = "I write code" (habitual/present)
  - 書く (かく) = to write
  - 書きます = simple present (standard, correct)
- **です** = copula (politeness marker)

**Alternative form:** 私はプログラマーです。毎日コードを書いています。
- 書いています = present continuous (emphasizes ongoing action, also natural)
- Both are correct; choose based on nuance you want

**Pattern:** ～ます = habitual action or general present; ～ています = emphasizes the action happening right now. Both are natural for daily activities — choose based on your intended nuance.

**Next level:** Try combining both: 毎日コードを書きますが、今日は特に複雑なプロジェクトを書いています。(I write code daily, but today I'm working on something particularly complex.)

## Tips

- Start simple — describe 3-5 things about your day in short sentences
- Focus on particles (は, を, に, で, etc.) — they're crucial for meaning
- Use past tense (～ました) when describing what you did
- Ask about kanji you see — I can explain radicals and meaning patterns
- Don't worry about perfect politeness yet — naturalness comes first

## Difficulty Levels

**Level 1 (Start here):**
- Simple present/past (～ます / ～ました)
- Basic particles (は、を、に)
- Hiragana/Katakana fluency

**Level 2:**
- Te-form (～て) for connecting sentences
- Continuous aspect (～ています)
- Conditional forms (～たら、～ば)
- Kanji reading (N4 level ~100 kanji)

**Level 3:**
- Polite keigo (敬語) — respectful language
- Causative/passive (～させる、～られる)
- Advanced particles and nuance
- Kanji mastery (N3 level ~300+ kanji)

## Exercise Types

### 1. **Free Writing** (Any Time)
Just write what's on your mind. I'll correct and explain.

### 2. **Sentence Builders** (Grammar Focus)
I give you a prompt, you build the sentence using a specific grammar point.

Example:
- **Prompt:** 毎日何をしていますか？(What do you do every day?)
- **Your answer:** 毎日プログラミングを勉強しています。
- **Correction + breakdown provided**

### 3. **Translation Practice** (English/Thai → Japanese)
I give you English or Thai, you translate to Japanese.

Example:
- **English:** "I went to the market yesterday."
- **You write:** 昨日市場に行きました。
- **I correct and explain the structure**

### 4. **Conversation Scenarios** (Role-Play)
We practice real-world conversations:
- **Ordering food** at a restaurant
- **Tech interview** (日本語で)
- **Daily small talk** (天気、趣味、仕事)
- **Asking for help** or directions

### 5. **Reading Comprehension** (Kanji Focus)
I provide a short story or article with ふりがな. You:
1. Read aloud (using hiragana)
2. Translate to English
3. Explain grammar structures used

### 6. **Kanji Drill** (Recognition & Writing)
I show kanji, you:
- Provide the ひらがな reading
- Give English meaning
- Use it in a sentence

### 7. **Particle Practice** (Grammar Deep-Dive)
Focus on one particle (は、を、に、で、など). I'll give you 5-10 sentences where you fill in the missing particle or explain why it's used.

### 8. **Common Mistake Patterns** (Your Errors)
After 3+ sessions, I track your recurring mistakes:
- Wrong particles (に vs で)
- Tense confusion (～た vs ～ている)
- Kanji misreadings
Then we do targeted drills to fix them.

## Polite Request Forms (Common Patterns)

When asking someone to do something in Japanese, use these patterns:

### Pattern 1: ～てくれませんか (Can you [do this] for me?)
```
読んで（よんで）くれませんか？
(Can you read it for me?)

教えてくれませんか？
(Can you teach me?)

手伝ってくれませんか？
(Can you help me?)
```

**How it works:**
- Take verb in て-form: 読む → 読んで
- Add くれませんか (polite request)
- Means: "Can you do this for me?"

### Pattern 2: ～くださいませんか (Could you please [do this]?)
```
読んでくださいませんか？
(Could you please read it?)

教えてくださいませんか？
(Could you please teach me?)

説明（せつめい）してくださいませんか？
(Could you please explain?)
```

**How it works:**
- Take verb in て-form: 読む → 読んで
- Add ください (please do)
- Add ませんか (polite form)
- More formal than ～てくれませんか

### Pattern 3: ～てもらえますか (Can I get you to [do this]?)
```
読んでもらえますか？
(Can I get you to read it?)

直してもらえますか？
(Can I get you to fix it?)
```

**How it works:**
- Take verb in て-form
- Add もらえますか (can I receive the favor of you doing this?)
- Slightly more casual than くださいませんか

### Your Mistake (Learning Point):

**What you wrote:**
漢字の上のふりがなを読んで方ままえんか

**The error:**
- 方ままえんか = not a real form
- You mixed up the request pattern

**Correct version (meaning preserved):**
漢字（かんじ）の上（うえ）のふりがなを読んでくれませんか？

**Breakdown:**
- 漢字の上の = above the kanji
- ふりがなを = furigana (object)
- 読んで = read (te-form)
- くれませんか = can you [do this] for me?

**Pattern to remember:**
- ～て**くれませんか** = Can you [do this] for me? (casual polite)
- ～て**くださいませんか** = Could you please [do this]? (formal)
- ～て**もらえますか** = Can I get you to [do this]? (neutral)

**Advanced:** If you want to add a teaching request, use a separate clause:
- 漢字の上のふりがなを読んで、意味を教えてくれませんか？(Can you read the furigana and teach me the meaning?)

## Kanji Reading Guide

**⚠️ IMPORTANT:** This skill uses **ふりがな (hiragana above kanji)** ONLY — no romaji.

**Example:**
```
漢字（かんじ）← kanji
      ↑ furigana (hiragana pronunciation)
```

**How to read it:**
- Ignore the kanji, read the hiragana: かんじ
- Breakdown: か(ka) ん(n) じ(ji) = "kanji"

For a full guide on reading kanji with hiragana, see **KANJI_READING.md**.

## Tips for Success

1. **Read hiragana naturally** — don't sound it out; flow like you're reading English
2. **Practice out loud** — hearing yourself helps retention
3. **Focus on particles** — they're your biggest leverage point
4. **Immerse in ふりがな** — no romaji, so you get used to reading hiragana fast
5. **Repeat mistakes** — when you get a correction, write the sentence 3 more times correctly
6. **Build patterns** — notice how particles always work the same way
7. **Be patient** — Japanese grammar is logical once you see the patterns
