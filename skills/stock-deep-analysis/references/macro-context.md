# Reference: Macro & Market Context

## Why Macro Matters for Stock Analysis

Individual stock performance is heavily influenced by macro environment.
Always overlay macro context when assessing valuation and growth outlook.

---

## Fed Rate Cycle → Sector Impact

| Rate Environment | Favored Sectors | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Rate hike cycle (rising) | Energy, Financials, Value stocks | Growth tech, REITs, Utilities |
| Rate cut cycle (falling) | Growth tech, REITs, Consumer Discretionary | Banks, Insurance |
| Rates stable (neutral) | Quality growth, Dividend growers | Highly leveraged companies |
| Inverted yield curve | Defensive (Healthcare, Staples) | Cyclicals, Small caps |

**Current Fed stance:** Search `Federal Reserve interest rate decision [current year]`

## Sector Rotation Framework

### Economic Cycle → Sector Leadership

| Cycle Phase | Leading Sectors | Lagging Sectors |
|---|---|---|
| Early expansion | Financials, Consumer Discretionary, Industrials | Utilities, Staples |
| Mid expansion | Technology, Materials, Energy | Financials |
| Late expansion | Energy, Materials, Healthcare | Consumer Discretionary |
| Recession | Utilities, Healthcare, Staples | Financials, Industrials, Tech |
| Recovery | Financials, Consumer Discretionary | Utilities, Staples |

**Identify current phase:** GDP growth trend + unemployment + PMI + yield curve shape

## Key Macro Indicators to Monitor

### Growth Indicators
* **GDP growth:** >2% = expansion | 0–2% = slowdown | <0% = recession
* **PMI (Purchasing Managers Index):** >50 = expansion | <50 = contraction
* **Unemployment rate:** Rising = economic stress | Falling = healthy

### Inflation & Rates
* **CPI (Consumer Price Index):** Target ~2%; >4% = Fed likely to hike
* **10Y Treasury yield:** Rising = headwind for growth stocks (higher discount rate)
* **Yield curve:** 10Y minus 2Y; negative = recession signal (historically 12–18 months lead)
* **Fed Funds Rate:** Current rate + forward guidance = key for valuation

### Market Sentiment
* **VIX (Volatility Index):** <15 = calm | 15–25 = moderate | >30 = fear | >40 = panic
  * High VIX = potential buying opportunity for quality stocks
  * Low VIX = complacency; watch for reversal
* **Put/Call ratio:** >1.0 = bearish sentiment | <0.7 = bullish/complacent
* **CNN Fear & Greed Index:** 0–25 = extreme fear | 75–100 = extreme greed

### Dollar Strength (DXY)
* **Strong USD:** Headwind for US multinationals (revenue in foreign currency)
* **Weak USD:** Tailwind for exporters, commodities, emerging markets

## Valuation Adjustment for Macro

### Interest Rate Impact on P/E
Higher rates → lower fair P/E (higher discount rate compresses multiples)

| 10Y Yield | Fair P/E Range (S&P 500) |
|---|---|
| <2% | 22–28x |
| 2–3% | 18–22x |
| 3–4% | 15–18x |
| 4–5% | 13–16x |
| >5% | 11–14x |

**Rule of thumb:** Each 1% rise in 10Y yield → ~10–15% compression in growth stock P/E

### Earnings Revision Cycle
* **Upgrades accelerating** → buy signal (analysts raising estimates)
* **Downgrades accelerating** → sell signal (estimates being cut)
* **Estimate revision breadth:** % of analysts raising vs cutting estimates
