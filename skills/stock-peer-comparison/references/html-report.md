# HTML Report Structure

## Skeleton

```html
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
<head>
  <meta charset="UTF-8">
  <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
  <title>Stock Peer Comparison Report</title>
  <script src="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/chart.js"></script>
</head>
<body>
  <!-- Header: report title, ticker list, data as-of date -->
  <!-- Section 1: Snapshot Table -->
  <!-- Section 2: Revenue & Net Income Charts -->
  <!-- Section 3: EPS Deep Dive -->
  <!-- Section 4: Valuation Comparison -->
  <!-- Section 5: Dividend Snapshot -->
  <!-- Section 6: News, MD&A & Guidance Summary -->
  <!-- Section 7: Peer Comparison Summary -->
  <footer>
    Data as of [date] | Generated by AI analysis | Sources: public information
    ⚠️ This report is for informational purposes only, not financial advice.
  </footer>
</body>
</html>
```

## Section Details

### Section 1 — Snapshot Table
Columns: Name | Sector | Market Cap | Latest Revenue (YoY%) | Latest Net Income (YoY%) | Latest EPS (YoY%) | Trailing P/E | Forward P/E | Dividend Yield

Formatting: comma separators and B/M suffix where appropriate, percent to one decimal, green for positive / red for negative, `N/A` in muted gray.

### Section 2 — Revenue and Net Income
- Chart 1: multi-line revenue (5Q)
- Chart 2: multi-line net income (5Q)
- Add 2–3 bullets highlighting divergences

### Section 3 — EPS Deep Dive
- Chart: EPS bars/lines across 5Q (consistent colors per ticker)
- Table: momentum + consistency labels
- Short paragraph comparing EPS quality and trend

### Section 4 — Valuation
- Grouped bar chart: trailing vs forward P/E
- Optional sector median line (if found)
- Short verdict table: cheap/neutral/expensive vs peers/sector

### Section 5 — Dividend Snapshot
- Card/row per stock: yield, payout, consistency
- If none: "No dividend"

### Section 6 — News, MD&A and Guidance
- 3–5 bullets per stock: (what changed) + (why it matters) + (source/date)

### Section 7 — Peer Comparison Summary
Include: strongest earnings momentum (and why), valuation verdict relative to growth and durability, management/guidance quality, income profile.

## Design System

Typography: system font stack, body 15–16px, line-height ~1.5
Theme: dark background, high-contrast text, cards with subtle borders and soft shadow
Chart colors: consistent per-ticker palette, stable across all charts
